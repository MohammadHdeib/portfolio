Process Plant

Emulsion Paints Plant Automation

Complete industrial automation and electrical design for a fully automated emulsion paint plant.
Process plant Complete automation design EPLAN & AutoCAD Remote I/O

Client: Al Quds Paints Industry

Location: Amman, Jordan

Industry: Chemicals / Paints

Date: December 2024

I was responsible for the complete design and engineering of the industrial automation system for a fully automated emulsion paint plant. The project scope covered the full control system lifecycle from concept to manufacturing-ready documentation.

Main responsibilities:

  • Electrical control panel design including layouts, schematics, power distribution, and component selection for MCCs and control panels.
  • Remote I/O architecture and cabinet layout to optimize wiring, diagnostics, and future expansion.
  • System topology design: network structure for PLCs, remote stations, HMIs, and higher-level systems.
  • Industrial communication integration using PROFINET and other protocols (EtherCAT, Modbus) where needed.
  • I/O addressing and mapping for PLC, remote I/O racks, and field instruments (valves, transmitters, motors).
  • Component specification for PLC CPUs, remote modules, VFDs, HMIs, power supplies, relays, and terminal blocks.
  • Preparation of documentation to support clean commissioning, troubleshooting, and future modifications.

The design focused on making the plant easy to operate and maintain: clear signal naming, logical grouping of I/O, and structured documentation so that both electricians and control engineers can work efficiently.
